Working Papers

  • Decoding Analyst Reports
    Haoyuan Li
    Abstract: Analysts are important information intermediaries. But the usefulness and biases of their recommendations have drawn questions. This paper tries to understand how analysts make recommendations using the textual information in analyst reports. By categorizing the qualitative information with a linguistic instrument (Word2Vec) in natural language processing literature, I show whether analysts make useful and biased recommendations depends on the categories of information they use to support their analyses in reports. Analysts' optimistic opinions toward new product and firm's non-fundamental information could partly explain why they recommend overvalued stocks (short-leg of anomalies). Overvalued stocks will not generate negative returns if analysts have optimistic opinions toward firm's growth opportunity and fundamentals. My findings advance the understanding of how analysts make recommendations and how they can contribute to the market efficiency from the perspective of information and information tones.

  • Information cycle and return seasonality
    Haoyuan Li and Roger Loh
    Abstract: Heston and Sadka (2008) find that the monthly cross-sectional returns of stocks depend on their historical same calendar-month returns. We propose an information-cycle explanation for this seasonality anomaly|that firms’ seasonal release of information coincide with higher returns during months with such dissolution of information uncertainty, and lower returns during months with no information releases. Using earnings announcements and changes in implied volatility as proxies for information releases, we find that seasonal winners in information-release months and seasonal losers in noninformation release months indeed drive the seasonality anomaly. Our evidence shows that scheduled information releases can give rise to the appearance of an anomalous seasonal pattern when stock returns are in fact responding rationally to information uncertainty.
    Presented at: SMU Finance Summer Camp, 2019; SFS Cavalcade Asia-Pacific 2019 (scheduled).
